  Jeff Luttrell
  jeffrey.luttrell@mavs.uta.edu

  500 W. First Street, Box 19023,
  Arlington, TX 76019                                      
  


Jeff Luttrell received his Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Mechanical Engineering from Texas Tech University.  He has extensive experience with aerospace thermal and thermodynamic analysis including  cooling systems, environmental control, electronics cooling, ventilation, icing, waste heat management, and root cause corrective action. He has interests in energy efficiency and energy integration and is currently working toward a doctoral degree in Mechanical Engineering with Dr. Dereje Agonafer in the area of thermal management for data centers using micro-encapsulated phase change material. 


Publications:

1. "The Effects of Surface Roughness on Convection with External Flow", ASME Proceedings of  the  30th 1995 National Heat Transfer Conference, vol. 8, August 1995, with J. Dunn

2. "F-22 Inlet and Duct Ice Detection and Accretion Test", AIAA Paper 98-0572, January 1998, with T. G. West

3. "F-22 Inlet Shed Ice Particle Sizing Test", AIAA Paper 2001-0091, January 2001, with T. G. West

4.  "Solar Assisted Household Clothes Dryer", Proceedings of ASME 2010 4th International Conference on Energy Sustainability ES2010-90095, May 2010, with D. Agonafer
